MAINTENANCE AND REPAIR

ON-VEHICLE SERVICE




Steering Column Cover

Removal Procedure

  1. Remove the lower steering column cover panel screw.
  2. Turn the steering wheel to expose the upper steering column cover panel screws. Remove the upper steering column cover panel screws and remove the upper and lower steering column cover panel.



  3. Remove the lower steering column cover panel from the cluster bezel.



Installation Procedure

  1. Install the lower steering column cover panel on cluster bezel.



  2. Install the upper and lower steering column cover panel screws.

    3. Tighten
    Tighten the upper and lower steering column cover panel screws to 1.5 N•m (13 lb-in).




Headlamp/Turn signal Switch and Lever

Removal Procedure

  1. Disconnect the negative battery cable.
  2. Remove the steering column cover. Refer to "Steering Column Cover" in this section.
  3. Remove the turn signal switch by pushing in on the tabs on the top and the bottom of the switch housing.
  4. Disconnect the electrical connections from the turn signal switch.



Installation Procedure

  1. Connect the electrical connections to the turn signal switch.
  2. Install the turn signal switch by snapping it into the switch housing.
  3. Install the steering column cover. Refer to "Steering Column Cover" in this section.
  4. Connect the negative battery cable.



Wiper/Cruise Control Switch and Lever

Removal Procedure

  1. Disconnect the negative battery cable.
  2. Remove the steering column cover. Refer to "Steering Column Cover" in this section.
  3. Remove the wiper switch by pushing in on the tabs on the top and the bottom of the switch housing.
  4. Disconnect the electrical connectors from the wiper switch. Disconnect the cruise control connector, if equipped.



Installation Procedure

  1. Connect the electrical connector to the wiper switch. Connect the cruise control connector, if equipped.
  2. Install the wiper switch by snapping it into the switch housing.
  3. Install the steering column cover. Refer to "Steering Column Cover" in this section.
  4. Connect the negative battery cable.



Steering Wheel

Tools Required

KM-210-A Steering Wheel Puller

Removal Procedure

  1. Disconnect the negative battery cable.
  2. Remove the SIR module. Refer to Section 8B, Supplemental Inflatable Restraints.
  3. Remove the steering wheel nut.



    4. Important : In order to install the steering wheel correctly, match-mark the steering column shaft to the steering wheel.

  4. Remove the steering wheel using the steering wheel puller KM-210-A.



Installation Procedure

  1. Align the match marks on the steering wheel and the steering column shaft. Turn the signal canceling cam on the wheel to the left.
  2. Install the steering wheel nut.

    3. Tighten
    Tighten the steering wheel nut to 41 N•m (30 lb-ft).

  3. Install the SIR module. Refer to Section 8B, Supplemental Inflatable Restraints.
  4. Connect the negative battery cable.

Installation Procedure

  4. Connect the negative battery cable.

Steering Angel Sensor

Removal Procedure

  1. Disconnect the negative battery cable.
  2. Remove the intermediate shaft from column shaft by removing the upper pinch bolt. Refer to "intermediate shaft,joint and dash seal" in this section.
  3. Disconnect the connector of steering angle sensor.
  4. Move the steering angle sensor upward to remove its key from retaining bracket.



  5. Remove the steering angle sensor by turning out of retaining bracket and sliding down on the column shaft.



Installation Procedure

  1. Slide up the steering angle sensor until placing to an opening of retaining bracket.
  2. Install the steering angle sensor on the groove of retaining bracket.
  3. Connect the connector of steering angle sensor.
  4. Connect the negative battery cable.
  5. Necessarily perform the steering angle sensor centering after installing the steering angle sensor. Refer to "Steering Angle Sensor Centering" in this section.

Steering Angel Sensor Centering

  1. Park the vehicle on the flat place.
  2. Perform the wheel alignment. Refer to Section 2B, Wheel Alignment.
  3. Position the steering wheel in a straight forward and turn the ignition ON.
  4. Connect the scan tool on the data link connector of vehicle.
  5. Select the ESP menu, that is the sub-menu of ABS system on the scan tool. And then perform the steering angle sensor centering.

  6. Check steering angle sensor centering by turning the steering wheel more than 5 degrees into left and right direction.
  7. Check and clear any related DTCs for the steering angle sensor.



Steering Column

    Important : Remove the steering column only if:

    • The steering column requires replacement.
    • The steering and the ignition lock housing require replacement.
    • Another operation requires the removal of the steering column.

    Notice : The steering column is extremely susceptible to damage after it has been removed from the vehicle. Dropping the column assembly on its end or hammering the end of the steering shaft can collapse the steering shaft or loosen plastic injections which maintain column rigidity. Leaning on the column can cause it to bend or deform. Any of the above damage can impair the column's collapsible design. If it is necessary to remove the steering wheel, use only the specified steering wheel puller.




Removal Procedure

  1. Disconnect the negative battery cable and let the vehicle sit for 1 minute to deactivate the airbag.
  2. Remove the steering wheel. Refer to "Steering Column Cover" in this section.
  3. Remove the headlamp/turn signal switch and lever. Refer to "Headlamp/Turn Signal Switch and Lever" in this section.
  4. Remove the wiper/cruise control switch and lever. Refer to "Wiper/Cruise Control Switch and Lever" in this section.
  5. Remove the clock spring. Refer to Section 8B, Supplemental Inflatable Restraints.
  6. Remove the driver knee bolster. Refer to Section 9G, Interior Trim.



  7. Remove the ignition switch. Refer to "Ignition Switch" in this section.
  8. Remove the key hole lamp. Refer to "Key Hole lamp" in this section.
  9. Remove the immobilizer module, if equipped. Refer to Section 9T2, Immobilizer Anti-Theft System.
  10. Remove the key cylinder. Refer to "Key Cylinder" in this section.
  11. Remove the SSPS control module. Refer to Section 6C, Power Steering Gear.
  12. Remove the steering shaft universal joint pinch bolt and the steering column assembly lower bracket bolt.
  13. Remove the steering angle sensor. Refer to "Steering Angle Sensor" in this section.



  14. Remove the steering column assembly lower bracket bolts.



  15. Remove the steering column assembly upper bracket bolts.
  16. Remove the steering column assembly from the vehicle.



Installation Procedure

    Important : For proper installation of the steering column, be sure the steering wheel spokes are centered diagonally and pointed downward, and that the front wheels are in the straight-ahead position.

  1. Install the steering angle sensor. Refer to "Steering Angle Sensor" in this section.
  2. Carefully guide the steering shaft into the universal joint.

    3. Important : Provide support for the steering column assembly until the mounting bolts are fastened. Do not let the steering column assembly hang unsupported.




  3. Install the pinch bolt into the non-threaded hole of the flange.

    4. Tighten
    Tighten the steering column shaft securing bolt to the universal joint pinch to 34 N•m (25 lb-ft).

  4. Install the steering column assembly lower bracket bolt and upper bracket bolts.

    5. Tighten

    Tighten the steering column assembly lower bracket bolt to 25 N•m (18 lb-ft).
    Tighten the steering column assembly upper bracket bolts to 22 N•m (16 lb-ft).



  5. Install the SSPS control module. Refer to Section 6C, Power Steering Gear.
  6. Install the key cylinder. Refer to "Key Cylinder" in this section.
  7. Install the immobilizer module, if equipped. Refer to Section 9T2, Immobilizer Anti-Theft System.
  8. Install the key hole lamp. Refer to "Key Hole lamp" in this section.
  9. Install the ignition switch. Refer to "Ignition Switch" in this section.
  10. Install the driver knee bolster. Refer to Section 9G, Interior Trim.
  11. Install the clock spring. Refer to Section 8B, Supplemental Inflatable Restraints.



  12. Install the wiper/cruise control switch and lever. Refer to "Wiper/Cruise Control Switch and Lever" in this section.
  13. Install the headlamp/turn signal switch and lever. Refer to "Headlamp/Turn Signal Switch and Lever" in this section.
  14. Install the steering wheel. Refer to "Steering Wheel" in this section.
  15. Connect the negative battery cable.

GENERAL DESCRIPTION AND SYSTEM OPERATION

Steering Wheel and Column

Caution : To ensure the energy-absorbing action of the steering column, it is important to use only the specified screws, bolts, and nuts, tightened to the specified torque.

In addition to the steering function, the steering column provides safety and security.
The energy-absorbing column is designed to compress in a front-end collision to lessen the chance of driver injury.
The ignition switch and the lock are mounted on the column, allowing the ignition and steering operations to be locked to inhibit theft of the car.
The column levers trigger the turn signals, the headlight beams, and the windshield washer and wipers.
A tilt steering column uses a spherical joint to allow the steering wheel to tilt up and down. This enables the driver to adjust the steering wheel to a comfortable position.

Notice : Apply a thin coat of lithium grease to all friction points when reassembling.

The column may be disassembled and reassembled easily.

Ignition Key Reminder

The ignition key reminder alerts the driver that the key is still in the ignition when the driver attempts to exit the vehicle.
An internal switch in the ignition lock cylinder supplies battery voltage to the reminder chime module when all of the following conditions are true:
  • The key is in the ignition switch.
  • The ignition is OFF.
  • The driver's door is open.
For information on removal and installation of the reminder chime module, refer to Section 9E, Instrumentation/Driver Information.
