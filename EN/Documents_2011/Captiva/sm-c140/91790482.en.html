Transmission Disassemble

Special Tools

    • DT-48179 Universal Driver Handle
    • DT-48182 Needle Sleeve Remover/Installer
    • DT-48183 Removal Bridge
    • DT-48186 Intermediate Shaft Remover
    • DT-48189 Differential Bearing Race Remover
    • DT-48225 Input Shaft Holder
    • DT-48226 Transaxle Housing Groove Ball Bearing Remover/Installer
    • DT-48227 Roller Bearing Remover/Installer
    • DT-48228 Clutch Housing Groove Ball Bearing Remover/Installer
    • DT-48229 Intermediate Shaft Remover
    • DT-48231 Input and Output Shaft Pusher/Puller
    • DT-48232 Input Shaft Synchro Body Protector
    • DT-48233 Intermediate Shaft Press Adapter


  1. Remove the transaxle from the vehicle. Refer to Transmission Replacement .
  2. Remove the bolts, the washer, and the shift control cable bracket.


  3. Remove the concentric slave cylinder retaining bolts.
  4. Disconnect the concentric slave cylinder pipe from the concentric slave cylinder.


    5. Note: When removing the bush, make sure not to remove it with excessive force.

  5. Remove the bush from the concentric slave cylinder pipe.
  6. Remove the concentric slave cylinder pipe.


  7. Remove the O-ring and the concentric slave cylinder.


    8. Note: Puncture the seal in the centre to prevent damage to the transaxle.

  8. Knock a hole in the sealing plug of the intermediate shaft with a suitable tool.


  9. Remove the intermediate shaft sealing plug and the oil pipe using a suitable tool. Discard the sealing plug and the oil pipe.


  10. Hold the input shaft with the DT-48225  (1).
  11. Remove the retainer plug from the intermediate shaft. Discard the retainer plug.

    12. Shift the transaxle into any gear.

  12. Remove the DT-48225 .


  13. Remove the transaxle rear cover bolts.


  14. Remove the transaxle rear cover and the oil pipe.


  15. Hold the input shaft with the DT-48225  (1).
  16. Remove the input shaft retainer plug and the output shaft retainer plug. Discard the retainer plugs.

    17. Shift the transaxle into any gear.

  17. Remove the DT-48225 .


  18. Remove the gear lever assembly.
    19. • Position the transaxle into neutral.
    • Remove the shift guide bolt (1).
    • Remove the shift retaining bolts.
    • Remove the select control lever (2).
    • Remove the gear lever assembly (3).


  19. Remove the reversing lamp switch and the washer from the transaxle housing.


  20. Remove the eight bolts from the clutch housing side.


  21. Remove the 12 bolts from the transaxle housing side.


  22. Remove the transaxle housing from the clutch housing.
    23. 22.1. Install the DT-48231 onto the transaxle housing.
            • Tighten the four bolts.
            • Screw the spindles into the input and output shaft.
    22.2. Separate the transaxle housing from the clutch housing.
            • Hold the spindles (1) using a suitable tool.
            • Turn via nuts (2) in direction of arrow.
    22.3. Remove the DT-48231 from the transaxle housing.
            • Unscrew the spindles into the input and output shaft.
            • Remove the four bolts.


    Note: This prevents the 3rd/4th synchro body assembly falling apart.

  23. Install the DT-48232  (1) and the retainer plug to the input shaft.


  24. Remove the input and output shaft assembly with the respective selector forks out of the clutch housing.

    25. Knock intermediate shaft out of intermediate shaft groove ball bearing using the DT-48186  (1).

    Note: Hold the input and output shaft assembly firmly.

  25. Remove the DT-48232 and the retainer plug from the input shaft.


  26. Remove the differential (1) and the output shaft roller bearing (2) from the clutch housing.


  27. Remove the magnet from the transaxle housing.


  28. Remove the drain channel from the transaxle housing.
  29. Remove the idle shift rail bush from the transaxle housing.
  30. Remove the idle shift rail from the transaxle housing.


  31. Remove the thread pin (3), the torsion spring (2), and the reverse shifting blocker (1) from the trans-axle housing. Discard the thread pin.


  32. Remove the needle sleeve from the transaxle housing using the DT-48179  (1) and the DT-48182  (2).


  33. Remove the input shaft and output shaft groove ball bearings from the transaxle housing using the DT-48179  (1) and the DT-48226  (2).


  34. Remove the differential bearing race from the transaxle housing.
    35. 34.1. Install the DT-48189 .
    34.2.  Turn the nut (1) in direction of arrow.
    34.3.  Install the DT-48183 to the DT-48189 .
    34.4.  Hold the spindle using a suitable tool.
    34.5. Turn the nut (2) in direction of arrow.


  35. Remove the shim from the transaxle housing.


  36. Remove the shaft seal ring from the transaxle housing. Discard the shaft seal ring.


  37. Remove the input shaft roller bearing from the clutch housing using the DT-48179  (1) and the DT-48227  (2).


  38. Remove the circlip from the Intermediate shaft groove ball bearing.


  39. Remove the Intermediate shaft groove ball bearing from the clutch housing using the DT-48179  (1) and the DT-48228  (2).


  40. Remove the differential bearing race from the clutch housing.
    41. 40.1. Install the DT-48189 .
    40.2. Turn the nut (1) in direction of arrow.
    40.3. Install the DT-48183 to the DT-48189 .
    40.4. Hold the spindle using a suitable tool.
    40.5. Turn the nut  (2) in direction of arrow.


  41. Remove the shim from the clutch housing.


  42. Remove the shaft seal ring (1) from the clutch housing. Discard the shaft seal ring.


    43. Note: Observe the orientation of the coiled pin before removal

  43. Remove the coiled pin (6). Discard the coiled pin.
  44. Remove the coiled pin (5). Discard the coiled pin.
  45. Remove the shift interlock (3).
  46. Remove the shift finger (4).
  47. Remove the outer spring seat (2).
  48. Remove the 5th/Reverse pressure spring (1).


  49. Remove the coiled pin (2). Discard the coiled pin.
  50. Remove the gear lever (1).
  51. Remove the vent cap (3).
  52. Remove the shift cover (4).


  53. Remove the circlips (1). Discard the circlips.
  54. Remove the inner spring seat (2).
  55. Remove the 1st/2nd pressure spring (3).
  56. Remove the angle disc (4) from the shift rod (5).


  57. Clean all components in a suitable solution.
  58. Inspect all components for wear or damage.


  59. Press off the 1st gear assembly and the 2nd gear assembly as a unit, from the intermediate shaft using the DT-48233  (1), and a hydraulic press.


  60. Put the 1st gear assembly and the 2nd gear assembly, as a unit, on the bench.
  61. Remove the thrust washer (1).
  62. Remove the roller bearing (2).
  63. Remove the 1st gear (3).
  64. Remove the roller bearing (4).
  65. Remove the bearing collar (5).
  66. Remove the thrust washer (6).


  67. Remove the 1st gear inner cone (1).
  68. Remove the 1st gear blocking ring (2).
  69. Remove the 1st gear outer cone (3).
  70. Remove the 1st/2nd synchroniser assembly (4).


  71. Remove the 2nd gear outer cone (1).
  72. Remove the 2nd gear blocking ring (2).
  73. Remove the 2nd gear inner cone (3).
  74. Remove the 2nd gear.


  75. Remove the needle bearings from the intermediate shaft.


  76. Remove the sleeve bearing using the DT-48233 and hydraulic press.


  77. Remove the thrust washer (1).
  78. Remove the 4th gear (2).
  79. Remove the needle bearing (3).
  80. Remove the 4th gear blocking ring (4).


  81. Remove the bearing collar and the 3rd/4th synchroniser from the input shaft using the gear and bearing separator plate under the 3rd speed gear, the DT-48234  (1), and a hydraulic press.


  82. Remove the following components as an assembly:
    83. • The bearing collar (1)
    • The 3rd/4th synchroniser assembly (2)
    • The thrust washer (3)
    • The thrust bearing (4)
    • The 3rd gear blocking ring (5)
    • The 3rd gear (6)


  83. Remove the needle bearing (1).
  84. Remove the thrust bearing (2).


  85. Press off 5th gear using the gear and bearing separator plate, the DT-48234  (1) and a hydraulic press.


    86. Note: The 4th driven gear is a very tight press.

  86. Press off the 4th driven gear using the gear and bearing separator plate, the DT-48234  (1) and a hydraulic press.


    87. Note: The 3rd driven gear is a very tight press.

  87. Remove the 3rd driven gear using the gear and bearing separator plate, the DT-48234  (1) and a hydraulic press.


  88. Remove the 5th gear (1) and the two needle bearings (2).


    89. Note: Do not contact the thrust washer under the reverse gear.

  89. Remove the bearing collar using the gear and bearing separator plate, the DT-48234  (1), and a hydraulic press.


  90. Remove the following components as an assembly:
    91. • The bearing collar (1)
    • The 5th gear blocking ring (2)
    • The 5th/Reverse synchroniser assembly (3)
    • The reverse blocking ring (4)
    • The reverse gear  (5)
    • The needle bearing (6)


       Note: Ensure that the gear and bearing separator plate is not making contact with the pinion gear before pressing off the collar and thrust washer.

    • Remove the collar and the thrust washer using the gear and bearing separator plate, the DT-48234  (1), and a hydraulic press.


  91. Inspect the clutch housing and the differential housing for the following:
    92. • Cracks
    • Porosity
    • Damaged mating surface
    • Stripped bolt threads
    • Distortion
    • Replace any part that exhibits any of these conditions.


  92. Inspect the gear teeth and the gear splines for excessive wear or damage.
  93. Remove minor nicks or scratches with an oil stone. Replace worn or damaged gears.


  94. Inspect the thrust washer for wear or damage. Replace worn or damaged thrust washers.


    95. Note: Do not allow the bearings to spin. Turn the bearings slowly by hand. Spinning bearings may cause damage to the rollers.

  95. Inspect the condition of all thrust bearings, input shaft bearings and output shaft bearings.
    96. • Wash the bearings thoroughly in a cleaning solvent.
    • Apply compressed air to the bearings.
    • Lubricate the bearings with light oil.
    • Check the bearing for roughness by slowly turning the race by hand.


  96. Clean the synchroniser with clean solvent.
  97. Air dry the components.
  98. Inspect the synchroniser teeth for the following conditions:
    99. • Wear
    • Scuffing
    • Nicks
    • Burrs
    • Breaking
  99. Inspect the keys or the springs for the following:
    100. • Wear
    • Cracks
    • Distortion


    • Inspect the selector forks, shafts and the rubber end for wear or damage.
      Replace any part that is worn or damaged.
    • If any of the following conditions exist, replace synchroniser.
