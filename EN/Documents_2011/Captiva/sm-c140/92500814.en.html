Circuit/System Description

The fuel temperature sensor is integrated within the fuel filter assembly. The fuel temperature sensor is a variable resistor that measures the temperature of the fuel. The engine control module (ECM) supplies 5 V to the signal circuit and supplies an ground to the low reference circuit. Conditions for Running the DTC
    • The ignition is ON or the engine is running.
    • The DTCs run continuously once the above condition is met.

Conditions for Setting the DTC

P0181 00

The fuel temperature and Inlet Air Temperature (IAT) 1 are more than 100°C (180°F) of each other at start-up.

or

The fuel temperature and IAT 1 are more than 10°C (18°F) of each other at start-up.

or

The IAT 1 has dropped more than 2.5°C (4.5°F) after running more than 6 min above 24 km/h (15 MPH).

P0182 00

The ECM detects that the fuel temperature sensor voltage is less than 0.127 V for 0.5 s.

P0183 00

The ECM detects that the fuel temperature sensor voltage is greater than 4.9 V for 0.5 s.

Action Taken When the DTC Sets

DTCs P0181 00, P0182 00, and P0183 00 are Type B DTCs.

Conditions for Clearing the DTC

DTCs P0181 00, P0182 00, and P0183 00 are Type B DTCs.

Diagnostic Aids
    • A short to a separate 5 V source may set a DTC P0183 00.
    • A skewed sensor may result in poor driveability concerns.

Circuit/System Verification
  1. Engine running, observe the DTC information with a scan tool. DTCs P0181 00, P0182 00, or P0183 00 should not set.
  2. Operate the vehicle within the conditions for running the DTC to verify the DTC does not reset. You may also operate the vehicle within the conditions that you observed from the freeze frame/failure records data.

Circuit/System Testing
  1. Ignition OFF, disconnect the harness connector at the E11 Fuel Heater.
  2. Test for less than 5 Ω between the low reference circuit terminal 3 and ground.
    3. ⇒If greater than the specified range, test the low reference circuit for an open/high resistance. If the circuit tests normal, replace the K20 Engine Control Module.
  3. Ignition On, test for 4.8 - 5.2 V between the signal circuit terminal 1 and ground.
    4. ⇒If less than the specified range, test the signal circuit for a short to ground. If the circuit tests normal, replace the K20 Engine Control Module.
    ⇒If greater than the specified range, test the signal circuit for a short to voltage or an open/high resistance. If the circuit tests normal, replace the K20 Engine Control Module.
  4. If all circuits test normal, replace the E11 Fuel Heater.
