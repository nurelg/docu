Crankshaft Replacement - LD9

Special Tools

    • EN-36792 Crankshaft Rear Oil Seal Installer
    • EN-45059 Angle Meter

For equivalent regional tools, refer to Special Tools : LD9 → LE5 .

Disassembly Procedure


    Note: Take extreme care to prevent any scratches, nicks or damage to the crankshaft.

  1. Remove the engine. Refer to Engine Replacement : LD9 → LE5 or LE9 .
  2. Remove the flywheel or flexible plate bolts.
  3. Remove the flywheel or flexible plate.


  4. Remove the crankshaft rear oil seal.
  5. Mount the engine assembly to an engine stand.


  6. Remove the crankshaft pulley bolts.
  7. Remove the crankshaft pulley.


  8. Remove the front timing belt cover bolts.
  9. Remove the front timing belt cover.


  10. Loosen the timing belt automatic tensioner bolt.
  11. Rotate the timing belt automatic tensioner hex-key clockwise to release the tension.
  12. Remove the timing belt idler pulley bolt and nut.
  13. Remove the timing belt idler pulleys.
  14. Remove the timing belt.
  15. Remove the engine mount adapter support bracket bolts.
  16. Remove the engine mount adapter support bracket.


  17. Remove the ignition wires from the spark plugs.
  18. Remove the camshaft cover. Refer to Camshaft Cover Replacement : LE5 or LE9 → LD9 .


    19. Note: Take extreme care to prevent any scratches, nicks or damage to the camshafts.

  19. While holding the intake camshaft firmly in place, remove the intake camshaft gear bolt.
  20. Remove the inlet camshaft gear.
  21. While holding the exhaust camshaft firmly in place, remove the exhaust camshaft gear bolt.
  22. Remove the exhaust camshaft gear.


  23. Remove the crankshaft gear .


  24. Remove the rear timing belt cover bolts and the cover.


  25. Rotate the engine on the engine stand (1).


  26. Remove the sump retaining bolts.
  27. Remove the sump.
  28. Remove the oil suction pipe bolts.
  29. Remove the oil suction pipe with O-ring.
  30. Remove the sump scraper bolts.
  31. Remove the sump scrapers.


  32. Remove the oil pump retaining bolts.
  33. Remove the oil pump.


  34. Mark the order of the connecting rod bearing caps for installation.
  35. Remove the connecting rod bearing cap bolts for all of the pistons.
  36. Remove the connecting rod bearing caps and the lower connecting rod bearings.


  37. Mark the order of the crankshaft bearing caps for installation.
  38. Remove the crankshaft bearing cap bolts.
  39. Remove the crankshaft bearing caps and the lower crankshaft bearings.
  40. Remove the crankshaft.
  41. Clean the parts if needed.

Assembly Procedure


  1. Coat the crankshaft bearings with engine oil.
  2. If replacing the crankshaft, transfer the reluctor wheel to the new crankshaft.


  3. Install the crankshaft.
  4. Install the lower crankshaft bearings in the bearing caps.
  5. Inspect the crankshaft end play with the crankshaft bearings installed.
  6. Check for permissible crankshaft end play. Refer to Engine Mechanical Specifications : LE5 .


    7. Note: Grease the crankshaft journals and lubricate the crankshaft bearings slightly so that the plastic gauging thread does not tear when the crankshaft bearing caps are removed.

  7. With the crankshaft mounted on the front and rear crankshaft bearings, check the middle crankshaft journal for permissible out-of-round (runout). Refer to Engine Mechanical Specifications : LE5 .


  8. Inspect all crankshaft bearing clearances using a commercially available plastic gauging (ductile plastic threads).
  9. Cut the plastic gauging threads to the length of the bearing width. Lay them axially between the crankshaft journals and the crankshaft bearings.

    10. Caution: Refer to Fastener Caution in the Preface section.

  10. Install the crankshaft bearing caps and the bolts. Tighten the bolts to 50 N·m (37 lb ft) plus 45 degrees and 15 degrees using EN-45059 meter .


  11. Remove the crankshaft bearing cap bolts and the caps.
  12. Measure the width of the flattened plastic thread of the plastic gauging using a ruler. (Plastic gauging is available for different tolerance ranges.)
  13. Inspect the bearing clearance for permissible tolerance ranges. Refer to Engine Mechanical Specifications : LE5 .


  14. Apply a bead of adhesive sealing compound to the grooves of the crankshaft bearing caps.
  15. Install the crankshaft bearing caps to the engine block.

    16. Note: Grease the connecting rod journals and lubricate the connecting rod bearings slightly so that the plastic gauging thread does not tear when the connecting rod bearing caps are removed.

  16. Tighten the crankshaft bearing caps using new bolts. Tighten the bolts to 50 N·m (37 lb ft) using a torque wrench. Use the EN-45059 meter (1) to tighten the crankshaft bearings another 45 degrees and 15 degrees.


  17. Inspect all of the connecting rod bearing clearances using a commercially available plastic gauging (ductile plastic threads).
  18. Cut the plastic gauging threads to the length of the connecting rod bearing width. Lay them axially between the connecting rod journals and the connecting rod bearings.
  19. Install the connecting rod bearing caps. Tighten the connecting rod bearing cap bolts to 35 N·m (26 lb ft) using a torque wrench. Use the EN-45059 meter to tighten the connecting rod bearing cap bolts another 45 degrees plus 15 degrees.


  20. Remove the connecting rod bearing caps.
  21. Measure the width of the flattened plastic thread of the plastic gauging using a ruler. (Plastic gauging is available for different tolerance ranges.)
  22. Inspect the bearing clearance for permissible tolerance ranges. Refer to Engine Mechanical Specifications : LE5 .


  23. Install the connecting rod bearing caps to the connecting rods.
  24. Tighten the connecting rod bearing caps using new bolts and EN-45059 meter (1).


  25. Install the oil pump.
  26. Install the oil pump retaining bolts and tighten to 10 N·m (89 lb in).


  27. Install the crankshaft balancer unit assembly.
  28. Install the crankshaft balancer unit assembly retaining bolts. Tighten the bolts to 20 N·m (15 lb ft) and turn the bolts another 45 degrees using EN-45059 meter .


  29. Install the sump scrapers.
  30. Install the sump scraper retaining bolts and tighten to 8 N·m (71 lb in).


  31. Install the oil suction pipe with the new O-ring.
  32. Install the oil suction pipe retaining bolts and tighten to 8 N·m (71 lb in).


  33. Coat the sealant on the sump mating surface.
  34. Install the oil pan.
  35. Install the sump retaining bolts and tighten to 10 N·m (89 lb in).


  36. Rotate the engine on the stand.
  37. Install the rear timing belt cover.
  38. Install the rear timing belt cover bolts.


  39. Install the crankshaft gear and the bolt. Tighten the bolt to 135 N·m (100 lb ft) plus 30 degrees plus 10 degrees using EN-45059 meter (1).


  40. Install the engine mount adapter support bracket and the bolts and tighten to 45 N·m (33 lb ft).
  41. Install the timing belt idler pulley.
  42. Install the timing belt idler pulley bolt and nut and tighten to 25 N·m (18 lb ft).


    43. Note: Take extreme care to prevent any scratches, nicks or damage to the camshafts.

  43. Install the inlet camshaft gear .
  44. Install the intake camshaft gear bolt while holding the intake camshaft firmly in place. Tighten the intake camshaft gear bolt to 50 N·m (37 lb ft) plus 60 degrees and 15 degrees usingEN-45059 meter .
  45. Install the exhaust camshaft gear .
  46. Install the exhaust camshaft gear bolt while holding the exhaust camshaft firmly in place. Tighten the exhaust camshaft gear bolts to 50 N·m (37 lb ft) plus 60 degrees and 15 degrees usingEN-45059 meter .


  47. Install the timing belt automatic tensioner and tighten the bolts to 25 N·m (18 lb ft).
  48. Install the timing belt. Refer to Timing Belt Replacement : LD9 .
  49. Adjust the timing belt tension. Refer to Timing Belt Inspection and Adjustment : LD9 .


  50. Apply a small amount of gasket sealant to the corners of the front camshaft caps and to the top of the rear cylinder head cover to the cylinder head seal.
  51. Install the camshaft cover. Refer to Camshaft Cover Replacement : LE5 or LE9 → LD9 .
  52. Connect the ignition wires to the spark plugs.
  53. Connect the breather hose and PCV hose to the cylinder head cover.


  54. Install the front timing belt cover.
  55. Install the front timing belt cover bolts and tighten to 6 N·m (53 lb in).
  56. Install the hoist to the engine support bracket and lift the engine assembly far enough to take the weight off the engine stand.
  57. Remove the engine from the engine stand.


  58. Install a new crankshaft rear oil seal using EN-36792 installer (1).


  59. Install the flywheel or flexible plate.
  60. Install the flywheel or flexible plate bolts and tighten to 65 N·m (48 lb ft). Use EN-45059 meter (1) to tighten the flywheel bolts another 30 degrees plus 15 degrees. For the automatic transaxle, tighten the flexible plate bolts to 45 N·m (33 lb ft).
  61. Install the engine. Refer to Engine Replacement : LD9 → LE5 or LE9 .
