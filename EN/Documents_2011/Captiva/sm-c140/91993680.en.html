Circuit/System Description

When the PRNDL is placed in the REVERSE position, a signal is sent to the body control module (BCM). The BCM then applies voltage through the reversing lamps relay control circuit to the BCK/UP lamp relay in the underbonnet fuse block, inside rearview mirror, and rearview camera module. The BCK/UP lamp relay then illuminates the reversing lamps.

Conditions for Running the DTC
    • The ignition is ON.
    • The reversing lamps must be commanded ON.

Conditions for Setting the DTC

The BCM detects a short to ground on the reversing lamp relay control circuit when the reversing lamps are being commanded ON.

Action Taken When the DTC Sets

The reversing lamps do not illuminate.

Conditions for Clearing the DTC
    • The conditions for setting the DTC are no longer present.
    • A history DTC clears after 100 malfunction-free ignition cycles.
    • The BCM receives the clear code command from the scan tool.

Circuit/System Verification

Ignition ON, activate the Reversing Lamps output control on the scan tool. The Reversing Lamps parameter in the data list should display On and the reversing lamps should illuminate.

Circuit/System Testing
  1. Ignition OFF, disconnect the underbonnet fuse block connector C 5, inside rearview mirror, and rearview camera module (if equipped) that are in the circuit.
  2. Ignition OFF, disconnect the BCM wiring harness plug C2 and test for a ground signal at the connector terminal 22.
    3. ⇒If the circuit exhibits a ground signal, repair the short to ground in the control circuit.
  3. Reconnect the inside rearview mirror and test the BCM wiring harness plug C2, terminal 22 again for ground.
    4. ⇒If the circuit now exhibits a ground signal of 20 ohms or less, replace the inside rearview mirror.
  4. Reconnect the underbonnet fuse block connector and test the BCM wiring harness plug C2, terminal 22 again for ground.
    5. ⇒If the circuit now exhibits a ground signal of 20 ohms or less, replace the underbonnet fuse block.
  5. If equipped, reconnect the rearview camera module and test the BCM wiring harness plug C2, terminal 22 again for ground.
    6. ⇒If the circuit now exhibits a ground signal of 20 ohms or less, replace the rearview camera module.
    ⇒If the circuit does not exhibit a ground, replace the BCM.
