Crankshaft Balancer Replacement - LD9

Special Tools

    • KM-470-B Angular Torque Gauge
    • KM-949 Measuring Device

For equivalent regional tools, refer to Special Tools : LD9 → LE5 .

Removal Procedure


  1. Remove the sump. Refer to Sump Replacement : LD9 → LE5 or LE9 .
  2. Remove the oil suction pipe with the O-ring.
  3. Remove the sump scraper.


  4. Remove the crankshaft balancer unit assembly retaining bolts.
  5. Remove the crankshaft balancer unit assembly and the shim.

Adjustment Procedure


  1. Align the mark on the crankshaft gear with the notch at the bottom of the rear timing belt cover.


  2. In this crankshaft position, the flattened side (arrows) of both balancer shafts must face downward (oil pan side) and must be on a horizontal line.


  3. Screw KM-949 measuring device (3) with long knurled bolt (1) into 1st balancer shaft (inlet side) and hand tighten. The measuring arm (5) must point in the 9 o'clock direction as shown in the illustration.
  4. Install the dial gauge holder and dial gauge (4) on the cylinder block.


  5. Place pre-tensioned spindle (1) of gauge on measuring arm of KM-949 measuring device . The spindle must be set precisely between the notch marks, square to the plane surface (2).


  6. Determine the left and right stops by turning the knurled bolt (2).
  7. Set the dial of the gauge to zero.
  8. Use the knurled bolt (2) to move the 2nd balancer shaft (exhaust side) back and forth. Again, simultaneously read off the tooth backlash from the gauge.
  9. The permissible tooth backlash is 0.02-0.06 mm (0.0008-0.0024 in).


  10. The tooth backlash must be measured in 4 different positions, turn the crankshaft further at the fastening bolt of the timing belt drive gear by 45 degrees in the engine rotational direction until the measuring arm (2) is at 6 o'clock.


  11. Then loosen the knurled bolt (3) and set the measuring arm at 9 o'clock again and repeat the measurement.
  12. If the value determined in one of the 4 measurements lies outside the tolerance 0.02-0.06 mm (0.0008-0.0024 in), the tooth backlash must be adjusted.


    13. Note: The next larger or smaller balancer shim alters the tooth backlash by 0.02 mm (0.0008 in).

    Example of selection of balancer shim: The installed balancer shim with the code "70" gave a tooth backlash of 0.08 mm (0.0031 in). If a balancer shim with the code "67" is now installed, the tooth backlash will be approximately 0.06 mm (0.0024 in).

    Only one balancer shim may be installed.

  13. Remove the balancer unit from the cylinder block/crankshaft bearing caps and remove with the balancer shim. The balancer shim has a number (code), for easy assignment. The tooth backlash can be adjusted by using a balancer shim with a different thickness.

Installation Procedure



    Note: 

       • Before installing the crankshaft balancer unit assembly, perform the crankshaft balancer unit adjustment for the proper operation.
       • Take care not to chip or dent teeth when meshing gear sets on assembly.

  1. Align the mark on the crankshaft gear with the notch at the bottom of the rear timing belt.


  2. Turn the balancer shaft until the flattened side (arrows) of both balancer shafts face downward (sump side) and are on a horizontal line.


  3. Install the selected balancer shim with the balancer unit to the cylinder block.

    4. Caution: Refer to Fastener Caution in the Preface section.

    Note: If the balancer unit has to be replaced, use the thickest balancer shim with the code "85" for the initial installation to ensure a tooth backlash in any case.

  4. Install the crankshaft balancer unit assembly retaining bolts. Tighten the crankshaft balancer unit assembly retaining bolts to 20 N·m (15 lb ft) and turn the bolts another 45 degrees using the KM-470-B gauge .
  5. Install the sump scraper. Tighten the sump scraper retaining bolts to 8 N·m (71 lb in).
  6. Install the oil suction pipe with the new O-ring. Tighten the oil suction pipe retaining bolts to 8 N·m (71 lb in).
  7. Install the oil pan. Refer to Sump Replacement : LD9 → LE5 or LE9 .
