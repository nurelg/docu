Captiva
   

Power Steering Pressure Pipe/Hose Replacement — 2.4L

Removal Procedure

    Note: The left-hand drive model is shown. The right-hand drive model is similar.

  1. Syphon the power steering fluid from the fluid reservoir.
  2. Place a drain pan under the vehicle in order to catch the power steering fluid.

    3. Caution: Refer to Power Steering Hose Disconnected Caution in the Preface section.



  3. Remove the fitting and the pressure pipe from the power steering pump outlet.


  4. Remove the pressure pipe bracket bolt from the generator.
  5. Raise and support the vehicle. Refer to Lifting and Jacking the Vehicle .
  6. Remove the front exhaust pipe. Refer to Front Pipe Replacement : LNQ → LF1 → LE5 .
  7. Remove the transaxle rear mount assembly. Refer to Transmission Rear Mount Replacement : AWD → FWD .
  8. If the vehicle has all wheel drive (AWD), remove the transfer case assembly. Refer to Transfer Case Assembly Replacement .


  9. Remove the fitting and the pressure pipe from the power steering gear inlet.
  10. Remove the pressure pipe from the vehicle.

Installation Procedure

    Caution: Refer to Installing Hoses without Twists or Bends Caution in the Preface section.



  1. Route the pressure pipe from the power steering pump to the power steering gear.

    2. Caution: Refer to Fastener Caution in the Preface section.

  2. Install the pressure pipe and the fitting to the power steering gear inlet and tighten the pipe fitting to 28 N·m (21 lb ft).
  3. If the vehicle has AWD, install the transfer case assembly. Refer to Transfer Case Assembly Replacement .
  4. Install the transaxle rear mount assembly. Refer to Transmission Rear Mount Replacement : AWD → FWD .
  5. Install the front exhaust pipe. Refer to Front Pipe Replacement : LNQ → LF1 → LE5 .


  6. Install the pressure pipe bracket bolt to the generator and tighten to 10 N·m (88.5 lb in).


  7. Install the pressure pipe and the fitting to the power steering pump outlet and tighten to 28 N·m (21 lb ft).

    8. Caution: Refer to Using Proper Power Steering Fluid Caution in the Preface section.

  8. Fill the fluid reservoir with power steering fluid. Refer to Checking and Adding Power Steering Fluid .

    9. Caution: Refer to Air in the Power Steering System Caution in the Preface section.

  9. Bleed the power steering system. Refer to Power Steering System Bleeding .
  10. Inspect for leaks. If there are leaks, correct the cause of the leaks. Refer to Power Steering Fluid Leaks .
  11. Lower the vehicle.
