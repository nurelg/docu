Control Valve Body Cover Replacement

Removal Procedure
  1. Raise the vehicle. Refer to Lifting and Jacking the Vehicle .
  2. Remove the left front wheel and tire assembly. Refer to Tyre and Wheel Removal and Installation .
  3. Remove the left splash shield. Refer to Front Wheelhouse Liner Replacement .
  4. Remove the front engine mount.
  5. Remove the transaxle oil cooler lines and seals from the transaxle. Refer to Transmission Fluid Cooler Pipe Seal Replacement .


  6. Remove the 9  TORX bolts (2) from the control valve body cover (1).

    7. Note: Do not pry with a tool on the transaxle control valve body cover, case or sealing surfaces.

  7. Remove control valve body cover.

Installation Procedure


  1. Clean any gasket material from the control valve body cover and inspect the gasket mounting surface.
  2. Install a 3 mm (0.125 in) bead of RTV sealer onto the control valve body cover. Refer to Adhesives, Fluids, Lubricants, and Sealers for the recommended sealer.


  3. Install the control valve body cover (1) onto the transaxle case.

    4. Note: Use threadlocker on ALL bolt threads. Refer to Adhesives, Fluids, Lubricants, and Sealers for the recommended threadlock material.

  4. Install the control valve body cover bolts (2) hand tight.

    5. Caution: Refer to Fastener Caution in the Preface section.



  5. Tighten the control valve body cover bolts to 13 N·m (115 lb in) in the sequence shown.
  6. Install the transaxle oil cooler lines and seals to the transaxle. Refer to Transmission Fluid Cooler Pipe Seal Replacement .
  7. Install the front engine mount.
  8. Install the left splash shield. Refer to Front Wheelhouse Liner Replacement .
  9. Install the left front wheel and the tyre assembly. Refer to Tyre and Wheel Removal and Installation .
  10. Lower the vehicle.
  11. Check the transaxle fluid level. Refer to Transmission Fluid Level and Condition Check .
