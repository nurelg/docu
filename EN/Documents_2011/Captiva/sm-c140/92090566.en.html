Captiva
   

Power Steering Fluid Reservoir Outlet Hose Replacement — 2.4L

Removal Procedure

    Note: The left-hand drive model is shown. The right-hand drive model is similar.

  1. Syphon the power steering fluid from the fluid reservoir.
  2. Remove the reservoir bolts.
  3. Place a drain pan under the vehicle in order to catch the power steering fluid. Use a rag in order to absorb any spilled fluid.

    4. Caution: Refer to Power Steering Hose Disconnected Caution in the Preface section.



  4. Loosen the hose clamp and remove the reservoir outlet hose from the reservoir.


  5. Loosen the hose clamp and remove the reservoir outlet hose from the power steering pump inlet.


  6. Remove the bolts from the brackets.
  7. Remove the reservoir outlet hose from the vehicle.

Installation Procedure

    Caution: Refer to Installing Hoses without Twists or Bends Caution in the Preface section.

  1. Route the reservoir outlet hose from the reservoir to the pump.

    2. Caution: Refer to Fastener Caution in the Preface section.



  2. Install the bolts to the brackets and tighten to 10N·m (88.5 lb in).


  3. Install the reservoir outlet hose and the hose clamp to the power steering pump inlet.


  4. Install the reservoir outlet hose and the hose clamp to the reservoir.
  5. Install the bolts to the reservoir and tighten to 9 N·m (80 lb in).

    6. Caution: Refer to Using Proper Power Steering Fluid Caution in the Preface section.

  6. Fill the reservoir with power steering fluid. Refer to Checking and Adding Power Steering Fluid .

    7. Caution: Refer to Air in the Power Steering System Caution in the Preface section.

  7. Bleed the power steering system. Refer to Power Steering System Bleeding .
  8. Inspect for leaks. If there are leaks, correct the cause of the leaks. Refer to Power Steering Fluid Leaks .
